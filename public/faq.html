Frequently asked questions

A pragmatic interview is one that tests one's ability to do a job at hand via an assignment that closely resembles a day-to-day task.

We at CoreSkills believe that this way it saves a lot of time and energy for everyone involved in the hiring process.

Here is how CoreSkills makes coding interviews pragmatic:

  1. For each of your open positions, we help you prepare a customized coding assignment using the CoreSkills challenge library.
  2. We provide your candidates will all the tools and support they need during their interviews so that most of them complete their assignments.
  3. Once the assignment is completed, we prepare a candidate skills profile that you get together with the candidate's code to help you make a better hiring decision.

As of today, CoreSkills allows you to conduct Backend and Fullstack coding interviews ranging from Junior to Senior roles. We're planning to expand on available types of challenges later in 2020.

As of today, the CoreSkills interiew is language-agnostic.

There are 2 main reasons for this. First, we believe that everyone should be able to understand what makes a pragmatic interview. And second, we would like to provide everyone with a chance to contribute to the definition of pragmatic.

The CoreSkills interview is supposed to replace most of your pre-onsite technical interviews. It gives you a clear picture of whether your candidate has the tech competence you're looking for. And additionally, it provides you with an understanding of what to follow-up on during the on-site interview.
